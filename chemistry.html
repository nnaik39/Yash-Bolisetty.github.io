Nomenclature
Some basic organic chemistry terms.
Definition A carbonyl functional group is a C-O double bond.
Properties A C-O double bond is polar. Polar molecules are very attracted to each other, so members of the group have a comparatively high meling point. Soluble in water.
Aldehydes and Ketones Aldehydes and ketones are both organic compounds in the carbonyl functional group. An aldehyde is on the end of a carbon chain, while a ketone is in the middle.

Aromaticity Rules
What makes a hydrocarbon smelly?
Aromaticity Rules
CYCLIC The last carbon connects to the first carbon to form a ring(see benzene.)
CONJUGATED Every atom in the ring must be able to have resonance. So an atom with four bonds is out.
HUCKEL'S RULE The hydrocarbon must have n pi electrons, where n is in the sequence 2,6,10,14...(4n+2).
FLAT Molecular geometry must be planar, so no tetrahedral carbons (sp3 orbitals), etc.

Aromaticity and MO Theory
The aromaticity rules can be restated in terms of p orbitals. Why do they work?
Aromaticity and MO Theory
Delocalization A delocalized pi bond is a pi bond where the electrons are free to move over more than two nuclei. Electron delocalization happens when p orbitals in a flat, cyclic structure overlap. Delocalized electrons are more stable because they spread energy over a wide area rather than keeping it bottled up in a small one.
Aromaticity For a molecule to be aromatic, there must be a shell of overlapping p orbitals.
