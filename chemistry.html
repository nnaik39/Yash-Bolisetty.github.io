Organic Chemistry and Watermelons

Nomenclature
Some basic organic chemistry terms.
Definition A carbonyl functional group is a C-O double bond.
Properties A C-O double bond is polar. Polar molecules are very attracted to each other, so members of the group have a comparatively high meling point. Soluble in water.
Aldehydes and Ketones Aldehydes and ketones are both organic compounds in the carbonyl functional group. An aldehyde is on the end of a carbon chain, while a ketone is in the middle.

Aromaticity Rules
What makes a molecule smelly?
Aromaticity Rules
CYCLIC The last carbon connects to the first carbon to form a ring(see benzene.)
CONJUGATED Every atom in the ring must be able to have resonance. So four single bonds are out.
HUCKEL'S RULE The molecule must have n pi bonds, where n is in the sequence 2,6,10,14...(4n+2).
FLATMolecular geometry must be planar, so no tetrahedral carbons (sp3 orbitals).
