Organic Chemistry in Watermelons
Nomenclature
Some basic organic chemistry terms.
Aldehydes and ketones are both organic compounds which have a carbonyl functional group (where a C is double-bonded to an O). A C-O double bond is polar, which gives members of the carbonyl functional group many useful properties! Polar molecules are very attracted to each other, so it's harder to pull them apart, meaning aldehyes and ketones both have a comparatively high melting/boiling point. They are also soluble in water. The difference between them is that an aldehyde is on the end of a carbon chain, while a ketone is in the middle of a carbon chain.
